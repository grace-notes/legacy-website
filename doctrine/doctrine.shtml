DOCTRINE 100 to 400

These four courses, Doctrine 100 to 400, provide a thorough introduction to the basic categorical Bible teaching needed to understand the Christian way of life.

Grace Notes doctrinal studies are organized into a graded curriculum, roughly corresponding to the phases a Christian goes through while growing in grace.

  1. Orientation to the Christian Life - While very little information is needed to become a Christian, it takes quite a bit of Bible reading and study to learn all the things which are part of a Christian's position in Christ. There are many things to be understood about what God the Father provides for each believer at the time of salvation.
  2. Stability in the Christian Life - After a person begins to learn the fundamentals of Christian living, he learns that growth in Christ leads to stability in all parts of his life. Doctrines important for this understanding include:
    • Occupation with Christ
    • Sin and Confession of Sin
    • Union with Christ (Positional Truth)
    • The Local Church and the Body of Christ
    • Armor of God
    • Authority
    • Faith-Rest
    • Edification
    • Grace Provision
    • Legalism
  3. Edification - Edification actually begins when a new Christian first picks up his Bible and starts reading, attending Bible teaching, and participating in a church's training ministry. Edification is a life-long process; a Christian is commanded to "grow in grace and in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ", continuously advancing in spiritual understanding.
  4. Production - Witness and Ministry - A person can witness for Christ right after being saved; so "production" can really begin right away. However, study is required for a Christian to be prepared for ministry to the unsaved, or for a teaching ministry to believers.
